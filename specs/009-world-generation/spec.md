# Feature Specification: World Generation

**Feature Branch**: `009-world-generation`

**Created**: 2026-07-14

**Status**: Draft

**Input**: User description: "I believe we are ready to begin working on a world generation module for cetools. Details can be found at https://evolvedexperiment.github.io/cepheus-srd/worlds.html."

## Clarifications

### Session 2026-07-14

- Q: Should world names be generated by default, or only supplied by the caller? → A: Generate names by default (to reduce referee workload); the caller may still override with a specific name.
- Q: What method should generate a default world name? → A: Hybrid—combine curated stems procedurally (a curated pool used as syllable/stem material assembled into invented, pronounceable names).
- Q: Must auto-generated names be unique within a generated subsector? → A: Yes—names are guaranteed unique within a single generated subsector (regenerate on collision).

## User Scenarios & Testing *(mandatory)*

### User Story 1 - Generate a single world profile (Priority: P1)

A referee needs a fully-formed world for their game. They ask cetools to generate one world and
receive its Universal World Profile (UWP): the classic one-line code describing Starport, Size,
Atmosphere, Hydrographics, Population, Government, Law Level, and Technology Level. Every value
respects the SRD's dice rules, modifiers, caps, floors, and inter-characteristic dependencies, so
the referee can drop the result straight into play without hand-checking it against the rulebook.

**Why this priority**: The UWP is the atomic unit of world building in the Cepheus Engine—every
other piece of world data (bases, trade codes, subsector maps) is derived from or attached to it.
A tool that produces a single correct UWP is already useful on its own and is the foundation every
later story builds on.

**Independent Test**: Generate a batch of worlds and verify each UWP: characteristics fall in
their SRD-valid ranges, all dependency rules hold (e.g. a Size-0 world has Atmosphere 0; a
Population-0 world has Government, Law Level, and Technology Level 0), and the rendered profile
string round-trips to the same values. Delivers a complete, playable world profile.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a request for one world, **When** it is generated, **Then** the result includes a
   Starport class, a Size, an Atmosphere, a Hydrographics value, a Population, a Government, a Law
   Level, and a Technology Level, each within its SRD-defined range.
2. **Given** a generated world whose Size is 0, **When** its profile is inspected, **Then** its
   Atmosphere is 0 and its Hydrographics is 0.
3. **Given** a generated world whose Population is 0, **When** its profile is inspected, **Then**
   its Government, Law Level, and Technology Level are all 0.
4. **Given** a world whose rolled Technology Level is below an SRD-required minimum for its
   conditions, **When** the profile is finalized, **Then** the Technology Level is raised to that
   minimum.
5. **Given** a generated world, **When** it is rendered, **Then** it produces the classic
   pseudo-hexadecimal profile string (e.g. `A867A9C-F`) with a hyphen before the Technology Level.

### User Story 2 - Complete system data and full profile line (Priority: P2)

Having a bare UWP, the referee wants the rest of the world data the SRD attaches to a system: how
many people precisely, what bases sit in the system, how many gas giants and planetoid belts, what
trade codes apply, whether travelers should be warned (Amber Zone), and the system's allegiance.
They ask cetools for a fully-described system and receive the complete world-data line as printed
on a subsector listing.

**Why this priority**: This is what turns a raw code into a usable game location—bases and gas
giants drive refueling and adventure, trade codes drive commerce, and the Amber flag drives player
caution. It is high value but strictly builds on the P1 UWP, so it comes second.

**Independent Test**: Generate fully-described systems and verify: the Population Modifier yields a
specific head-count consistent with the Population code; base presence follows the starport-based
rules; trade codes match the UWP-to-trade-code table exactly; and the rendered full line contains
the profile, base code, trade codes, travel-zone flag, the Population-modifier/Belts/Gas-giants
(PBG) triple, and an allegiance.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a world with Population greater than 0, **When** its Population Modifier is generated,
   **Then** the modifier is between 1 and 10 and the derived head-count equals the modifier times
   ten raised to the Population code.
2. **Given** a world with a Class-A or Class-B starport, **When** bases are determined, **Then** a
   naval base may be present; **and** a scout base is never assigned to a Class-E or Class-X
   starport world.
3. **Given** a Size-0 world, **When** the system is generated, **Then** at least one planetoid belt
   is present.
4. **Given** a world whose UWP matches a trade classification (e.g. Atmosphere 4–9, Hydrographics
   4–8, Population 5–7 for Agricultural), **When** trade codes are assigned, **Then** the
   corresponding code(s) appear and non-matching codes do not.
5. **Given** a world with Atmosphere 10 or greater, a Government of 0, 7, or 10, or a Law Level of
   0 or 9+, **When** the travel zone is evaluated, **Then** the world is flagged as an Amber Zone
   candidate.
6. **Given** a fully-described system, **When** it is rendered, **Then** the output line contains
   the world name, hex location, profile, base code, trade codes/remarks, travel-zone code, PBG
   triple, and allegiance.

### User Story 3 - Generate a subsector of worlds (Priority: P3)

A referee starting a new campaign region wants a whole map's worth of worlds at once. They ask
cetools to generate a subsector; it walks the 8-column by 10-row hex grid, decides which hexes
contain a world, and produces a full system profile for each occupied hex, returning a subsector
listing keyed by hex coordinate.

**Why this priority**: A batch subsector is a major convenience for referees but is purely an
aggregation of P1/P2 world generation over a grid with a presence check. It delivers no new
world-level rules, so it is the lowest priority and can ship after single-world generation is
solid.

**Independent Test**: Generate a subsector and verify the grid is 8×10, each hex is independently
checked for world presence at the configured density, and every occupied hex carries a valid,
fully-described system whose hex coordinate matches its position. Delivers a ready-to-use region.

**Acceptance Scenarios**:

1. **Given** a request for a subsector at default density, **When** it is generated, **Then** each
   of the 80 hexes is independently checked and roughly half contain a world.
2. **Given** a subsector density modifier (e.g. rift, sparse, or dense), **When** the subsector is
   generated, **Then** the proportion of occupied hexes shifts accordingly.
3. **Given** a generated subsector, **When** each occupied hex is inspected, **Then** it contains a
   valid full system profile and its recorded hex coordinate matches its column/row position.
4. **Given** a generated subsector, **When** the world names are compared, **Then** every
   auto-generated name is unique within the subsector.

---

### Edge Cases

- **Asteroid (Size 0)**: Atmosphere and Hydrographics are forced to 0, an Asteroid/Vacuum trade
  code applies, and a planetoid belt is guaranteed in the system.
- **Uninhabited (Population 0)**: Government, Law Level, and Technology Level are 0, the Population
  Modifier is 0, and Barren-type trade codes may apply.
- **Value ceilings and floors**: Atmosphere never exceeds 15, Hydrographics stays within 0–10,
  Population never exceeds 10, Government never exceeds 15, and Law Level and Technology Level never
  drop below 0, regardless of dice and modifiers.
- **Hydrographics suppression**: a Size 0 or 1 world always has Hydrographics 0; low/none/corrosive
  atmospheres and thin-low atmospheres reduce the value but not below 0.
- **Technology Level minimums**: when conditions demand a higher minimum TL than was rolled, the TL
  is raised to the minimum rather than left low.
- **Base exclusions**: scout bases are excluded on Class-E/Class-X starports; a pirate base cannot
  coexist with a Class-A starport or a naval base in the same system.
- **Starport X**: a world with no starport still generates a valid (if minimal) profile and correct
  base/trade outcomes.

## Requirements *(mandatory)*

### Functional Requirements

#### Core UWP (P1)

- **FR-001**: System MUST generate a world's Size by rolling 2D6−2, yielding a value from 0 to 10.
- **FR-002**: System MUST generate Atmosphere as 2D6−7 plus Size, forcing Atmosphere to 0 when Size
  is 0, and capping Atmosphere at 15.
- **FR-003**: System MUST generate Hydrographics as 2D6−7 plus Size with the SRD size/atmosphere
  modifiers applied, forcing Hydrographics to 0 for Size 0 or 1, and clamping the result to 0–10.
- **FR-004**: System MUST generate Population as 2D6−2 with the SRD size/atmosphere/hydrographics
  modifiers applied, clamped to 0–10.
- **FR-005**: System MUST generate Government as 2D6−7 plus Population, forcing Government to 0 when
  Population is 0, and clamping to 0–15.
- **FR-006**: System MUST generate Law Level as 2D6−7 plus Government, forcing Law Level to 0 when
  Government is 0, and clamping to a minimum of 0.
- **FR-007**: System MUST determine the primary Starport class from 2D6−7 plus Population using the
  SRD Primary Starport table.
- **FR-008**: System MUST generate Technology Level as 1D6 plus the SRD tech-level DMs derived from
  Starport, Size, Atmosphere, Hydrographics, Population, and Government, never below 0.
- **FR-009**: System MUST raise a world's Technology Level to any SRD-mandated minimum implied by
  its Hydrographics, Population, and Atmosphere conditions.
- **FR-010**: System MUST render a world's core characteristics as the classic pseudo-hexadecimal
  UWP profile string in the SRD order (Starport, Size, Atmosphere, Hydrographics, Population,
  Government, Law Level, hyphen, Technology Level), using letters for values above 9.

#### System data and full line (P2)

- **FR-011**: System MUST generate a Population Modifier of 2D6−2 (minimum 1 when Population is
  above 0, else 0) and MUST be able to report the specific head-count as the modifier times ten
  raised to the Population code.
- **FR-012**: System MUST determine planetoid-belt presence (2D6, 4+), the belt count when present
  (1D6−3, minimum 1), and MUST guarantee at least one belt when the primary world is Size 0.
- **FR-013**: System MUST determine gas-giant presence (2D6, 5+) and the gas-giant count when
  present (1D6−2, minimum 1).
- **FR-014**: System MUST determine naval, scout, and pirate bases per the SRD rules, including the
  scout-base starport DMs and the exclusions (no scout base on Class-E/X, no pirate base alongside
  a Class-A starport or a naval base), and MUST reduce them to the SRD base code.
- **FR-015**: System MUST assign all applicable trade codes by matching the world's UWP against the
  SRD trade-classification table.
- **FR-016**: System MUST flag a world as an Amber Zone candidate when its Atmosphere is 10+, its
  Government is 0, 7, or 10, or its Law Level is 0 or 9+.
- **FR-017**: System MUST attach an allegiance to a system, defaulting to non-aligned (`Na`) when
  none is specified.
- **FR-018**: System MUST render a fully-described system as the SRD world-data line: world name,
  hex location, UWP profile, base code, trade codes and remarks, travel-zone code, the
  Population-modifier/Belts/Gas-giants (PBG) triple, and allegiance.

#### Subsector (P3)

- **FR-019**: System MUST generate a subsector as an 8-column by 10-row hex grid, independently
  checking each hex for world presence.
- **FR-020**: System MUST default world presence to a 50% per-hex chance and MUST support a density
  modifier (rift −2, sparse −1, dense +1) that adjusts the presence chance.
- **FR-021**: System MUST generate a full system profile for every occupied hex and record each
  system's hex coordinate (column then row).

#### Cross-cutting

- **FR-022**: World generation MUST be reproducible: given the same seed, the system MUST produce
  the identical world, system, or subsector.
- **FR-023**: The world-generation rules MUST be usable directly as a library, independently of any
  command-line or other delivery layer.
- **FR-024**: The system MUST expose world generation through the existing command-line tool as a
  world subcommand that prints results to standard output.
- **FR-025**: The system MUST generate a world name by default so the referee receives a named
  world without extra effort; the caller MAY override the generated name with a specific one.
- **FR-026**: Default world names MUST be produced by combining a curated pool of stems
  procedurally into invented, pronounceable names (a hybrid word-list/syllable approach), yielding
  enough variety that a full subsector does not visibly repeat.
- **FR-027**: When generating a subsector, auto-generated world names MUST be unique within that
  subsector; on a name collision the system MUST regenerate until the name is unique.

### Key Entities *(include if feature involves data)*

- **World**: the primary world of a system. Attributes: name (generated by default, caller-overridable), Size, Atmosphere,
  Hydrographics, Population, Government, Law Level, Starport class, Technology Level, Population
  Modifier, trade codes, travel-zone flag.
- **System**: a world plus its stellar surroundings. Attributes: the World, planetoid-belt count,
  gas-giant count, bases (naval/scout/pirate) reduced to a base code, allegiance, hex location.
- **Universal World Profile (UWP)**: the rendered representation of a World/System—both the
  classic profile string and the full world-data line.
- **Trade Code**: a two-letter classification derived deterministically from a world's UWP.
- **Travel Zone**: a safety classification (green/none, Amber, Red) attached to a system, with
  Amber assigned by rule and Red reserved for referee discretion.
- **Subsector**: an 8×10 grid of hexes, each optionally holding one System.

## Success Criteria *(mandatory)*

### Measurable Outcomes

- **SC-001**: 100% of generated worlds have every characteristic within its SRD-valid range
  (Size 0–10, Atmosphere 0–15, Hydrographics 0–10, Population 0–10, Government 0–15, Law Level ≥ 0,
  Technology Level ≥ 0).
- **SC-002**: 100% of generated worlds satisfy every SRD inter-characteristic dependency (Size-0 ⇒
  Atmosphere 0 and Hydrographics 0; Size 0/1 ⇒ Hydrographics 0; Population-0 ⇒ Government, Law
  Level, and Technology Level 0; TL raised to any mandated minimum).
- **SC-003**: Trade-code assignment matches the SRD trade-classification table for 100% of a
  reference set of hand-worked worlds covering every classification.
- **SC-004**: Base, planetoid-belt, and gas-giant presence rules produce outcomes within their
  SRD-defined probability bounds across a large generated sample (e.g. gas-giant presence ≈ 72% at
  the 5+ threshold), and never violate the exclusion rules.
- **SC-005**: Regenerating with the same seed reproduces an identical world, system, and subsector
  100% of the time.
- **SC-006**: A referee can generate a single fully-described world and read its complete world-data
  line in one command, and can generate a full 8×10 subsector in one command.
- **SC-007**: Over a default-density subsector sample, the share of occupied hexes is
  approximately 50%, shifting measurably up for dense and down for sparse/rift densities.
- **SC-008**: 100% of generated worlds have a non-empty name by default, and every auto-generated
  name within a single generated subsector is unique.

## Assumptions

- **Authoritative source**: All rules, tables, DMs, caps, and terminology come from the Cepheus
  Engine SRD Chapter 12 (Worlds); where the SRD leaves a step to referee discretion, that step is
  treated as caller-supplied rather than randomly generated.
- **Referee-discretion elements are out of automated scope**: Red Zone assignment, polity/allegiance
  boundaries beyond a default non-aligned value, communications routes, trade routes, and the
  drawing of subsector maps are the referee's to set; the module may accept them as inputs but does
  not invent them. Amber Zone flagging *is* rule-driven and is included.
- **World names are generated by default**: the SRD defines no world-naming algorithm, so cetools
  supplies one to reduce referee workload—a hybrid generator that assembles curated stems into
  invented, pronounceable names. Callers may override with a specific name, and names are unique
  within a generated subsector.
- **Seeding for reproducibility**: generation is driven through the project's existing chance seam,
  so a supplied seed yields deterministic output for testing and for referees who want repeatable
  results.
- **Delivery layer**: the feature reuses the existing command-line tool, adding a world subcommand
  alongside the existing character commands; the generation logic lives in the engine library so it
  is reusable by any future delivery layer.
- **Pseudo-hex rendering**: values above 9 are rendered with the project's existing
  pseudo-hexadecimal codec, consistent with how character data is already rendered.
- **Scope boundary for v1**: single-world (P1) and full-system (P2) generation are the core
  deliverable; subsector generation (P3) is included but may be delivered after the single-world
  path is complete.
